Slay the Spire Fan Page

The Ironclad

The Ironclad is the first playable character in Slay the Spire. He wields an arsenal of powerful strikes, boasts formidable defensive options, and can draw fiendish power from his demonic benefactors. He starts with 80 hp, highest of the playable characters

The Silent

The Silent is the second playable character in Slay the Spire. She is themed on a rogue fantasy build using poisons and daggers to move through the spire. She starts with a relatively low 70 hp

The Defect

The Defect is the third playable character in Slay the Spire. Using cards and Relics, The Defect Channels elemental spheres into a set of Orb slots. The Defect starts with three Orb slots and 75 hp.

Quick Player Guide

Slay the spire is a rogue-like game. Don't be upset if you cant slay the spire on the first try. As you progress in the game, you player will unlock new cards and relics which may provide powerful bonuses for your run. Collecting relics and upgrading cards are the best way for a player to go far in a particular run. There are three main Acts in the game the player will have to endure, each with their own unique set of bosses. Certain bosses can be countered with specific stratgies or tactics so be sure to know which boss you will be facing at the end of your Act.

Character playstyles will vary from run to run. Some runs will be filled with cards that combo well together but will fail against a certain opponent. Runs generally cannot be mimicked but can be quite similar. Don't try to rebuild the epic combo deck you may have gotten on a previous run. Just use what you are given. Note that cards and relics do not always have to be collected at the end of a floor. The player can pick and choose what they feel is best. A good strategy for players looking to save health is by picking the route with the most ? rooms as they have a lower chance of combat. A good strategy for players looking to collect relics is to pick the route with the most elite combats since elittes are guarenteed to drop a relic at the end of combat. Elites are much more powerful than normal enemies, so this strategy is not for the faint of heart.
